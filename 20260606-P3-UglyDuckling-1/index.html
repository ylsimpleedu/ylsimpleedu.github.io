P3：《丑小鸭》第一课

Posted on 2026-07-20 Views: Disqus:

第一节课文・Session 1 Reading Text

IT was so beautiful in the country. It was the summer time. The wheat fields were golden, the oats were green, and the hay stood in great stacks in the green meadows. The stork paraded about among them on his long red legs, chattering away in Egyptian, the language he had learned from his lady mother. All around the meadows and cornfields grew thick woods, and in the midst of the forest was a deep lake. Yes, it was beautiful, it was delightful in the country.

乡村的夏天多么美丽啊！麦田一片金黄，燕麦一片碧绿，青草地上堆着一垛垛干草。一只鹳鸟迈着它那长长的红腿，在草地上踱来踱去，用从妈妈那里学来的埃及话，叽叽喳喳地说个不停。草地和麦田四周都是茂密的树林，树林深处有一个幽静的深湖。是的，乡村美极了，美得让人心里欢喜。

In a sunny spot stood a pleasant old farmhouse circled all about with deep canals; and from the walls down to the water’s edge grew great burdocks, so high that under the tallest of them a little child might stand upright. The spot was as wild as if it had been in the very center of the thick wood.

在向阳的一角，静静立着一座老农舍，四周都是深深的河沟。从墙根到水边，长着高高的牛蒡草，最高的那棵下面，一个小孩子站进去都绰绰有余。这个地方野得像是深林的中心一样。

In this snug retreat sat a duck upon her nest, watching for her young brood to hatch; but the pleasure she had felt at first was almost gone; she had begun to think it a wearisome task, for the little ones were so long coming out of their shells, and she seldom had visitors. The other ducks liked much better to swim about in the canals than to climb the slippery banks and sit under the burdock leaves to have a gossip with her. It was a long time to stay so much by herself.

就在这个僻静的地方，一只鸭妈妈坐在巢里，等着小宝宝们破壳而出。可是，她最初的那份喜悦已经消失得差不多了。她开始觉得这是一件累人的差事：小家伙们迟迟不肯出来，来看望她的访客也寥寥无几。别的鸭子们更喜欢在河沟里游来游去，可不愿意爬上湿滑的河岸，钻到牛蒡叶下和她聊天。就这样，她独自坐了好长好长时间。

At length, however, one shell cracked, and soon another, and from each came a living creature that lifted its head and cried “Peep, peep.”

“Quack, quack!” said the mother; and then they all tried to say it, too, as well as they could, while they looked all about them on every side at the tall green leaves. Their mother allowed them to look about as much as they liked, because green is good for the eyes.

终于，一个蛋壳裂了！接着，又是一个。一个个小生命从蛋壳里钻了出来，抬起头，叫着” 啾啾！”

“呱呱！” 鸭妈妈应道。

于是，小家伙们模仿着鸭妈妈的叫声，也都尽力叫了起来。同时，他们好奇地四处张望着那些高高的绿叶。鸭妈妈任由他们东看西看，因为绿色对眼睛好。

“What a great world it is, to be sure,” said the little ones, when they found how much more room they had than when they were in the eggshell.

“Is this all the world, do you imagine?” said the mother. “Wait till you have seen the garden. Far beyond that it stretches down to the pastor’s field, though I have never ventured to such a distance. Are you all out?” she continued, rising to look. “No, not all; the largest egg lies there yet, I declare. I wonder how long this business is to last. I’m really beginning to be tired of it;” but for all that she sat down again.

“这个世界真大啊！” 小家伙们说，他们发现外面的空间比蛋壳里大多了。

“你们以为这就是全世界了吗？” 妈妈说，” 等你们见到花园就知道了。再往那边走，还能走到牧师家的田地呢 —— 不过我自己从没去过那么远。你们都出来了吗？” 她站起来看了看，” 不，还有没出来的 —— 最大的那个蛋还在那儿呢。不知道还要等多久，我真的开始厌倦了。” 尽管这么说，她还是重新坐了下来。

“Well, and how are you to-day?” quacked an old duck who came to pay her a visit.

“There’s one egg that takes a deal of hatching. The shell is hard and will not break,” said the fond mother, who sat still upon her nest. “But just look at the others. Have I not a pretty family? Are they not the prettiest little ducklings you ever saw? They are the image of their father—the good for naught! He never comes to see me.”

“Let me see the egg that will not break,” said the old duck. “I’ve no doubt it’s a Guinea fowl’s egg. The same thing happened to me once, and a deal of trouble it gave me, for the young ones are afraid of the water. Let me take a look at it. Yes, I am right; it’s a Guinea fowl, upon my word; so take my advice and leave it where it is. Come to the water and teach the other children to swim.”

“I think I will sit a little while longer,” said the mother. “I have sat so long, a day or two more won’t matter.”

“Very well, please yourself,” said the old duck, rising; and she went away.

“你今天感觉怎么样？” 一只老鸭子呱呱叫着，来探望鸭妈妈了。

“有一个蛋要等的时间特别长，蛋壳硬得很，一直不破，” 坐在巢里的鸭妈妈说，” 不过你看看其他的，我的孩子们多漂亮啊！你见过这么好看的小鸭子吗？他们简直和他们爸爸一个模子 —— 那个没用的东西！他从来不来看我。”

“让我看看那个不肯破壳的蛋，” 老鸭子说，” 那准是珍珠鸡的蛋。我自己也有过这样的经历，麻烦极了，因为珍珠鸡的雏鸟怕水。让我看看 —— 是的，没错，这就是珍珠鸡的蛋，我敢保证。听我的劝，把它丢在那里别管它了，带着其他的孩子去水里教他们游泳吧。”

“我想再坐一会儿，” 鸭妈妈说，” 我已经坐了这么久了，再等一两天又有什么关系呢。”

“随你便，” 老鸭子站起来说道，便离开了。

At last the great egg broke, and the latest bird cried “Peep, peep,” as he crept forth from the shell. How big and ugly he was! The mother duck stared at him and did not know what to think. “Really,” she said, “this is an enormous duckling, and it is not at all like any of the others. I wonder if he will turn out to be a Guinea fowl. Well, we shall see when we get to the water—for into the water he must go, even if I have to push him in myself.”

终于，那个最大的蛋裂开了，最后一只雏鸟啾啾叫着，从蛋壳里慢慢爬出来。他是那么高大、那么丑啊！鸭妈妈盯着他，不知道该怎么想。” 真是的，” 她说，” 这只鸭子也太大了，一点儿都不像其他的。难道他真的是珍珠鸡？好吧，等我们到水边就知道了 —— 不管怎样，他一定要下水，就算我必须亲手把他推进去。”

On the next day the weather was delightful. The sun shone brightly on the green burdock leaves, and the mother duck took her whole family down to the water and jumped in with a splash. “Quack, quack!” cried she, and one after another the little ducklings jumped in. The water closed over their heads, but they came up again in an instant and swam about quite prettily, with their legs paddling under them as easily as possible; and the ugly gray-coat was also in the water, swimming with them.

“Oh,” said the mother, “that is not a Guinea fowl. See how well he uses his legs, and how erect he holds himself! He is my own child, and he is not so very ugly after all, if you look at him properly. Quack, quack! come with me now.”

第二天，天气格外晴朗。阳光照在绿色的牛蒡叶上，鸭妈妈带着全家来到水边，” 扑通” 一声跳进水里。” 呱呱！” 她叫着，小鸭子们一只接一只地跳了进去。水一下子没过了他们的头，但他们转眼间就浮了上来，两条小腿在水里轻轻划动，游得十分好看，毫不费力；那只灰色的丑小鸭也在水里，和兄弟姐妹们一起游着。

“哦，” 鸭妈妈说，” 这不是珍珠鸡。你看他两条腿划得多好，姿势多挺拔！这是我自己的孩子。只要你好好看他，他其实也没那么丑嘛。呱呱！跟我来吧。”

课堂活动・In-Class Activities

活动 A：谁在告诉你你不够好？
从丑小鸭被嫌弃丑开始，聊一聊” 好看” 的标准是怎么来的 —— 哪些是天生的审美，哪些是被广告” 教” 出来的。

活动 B：你认识的与众不同的人
想一个曾经因为与众不同而被误解的人（真实的人或故事角色），和 AI 一起做一张” 与众不同档案卡”。

活动 C：我的与众不同宣言
找到自己最特别的一个特质或兴趣，和 AI 一起写成一段属于自己的” 个人宣言”，大声念出来。
