1.1 Science is Part of Daily Life

What is Science?

  1. Science is the knowledge of a discipline that involves systematic observations and investigative experiments on natural phenomena.

  2. The word science comes from the Latin word “scientia” which means knowledge.

  3. Natural phenomena are daily activities or events happening in nature that are not man-made.

  1. For example,

    Phenomena Examples
    Growth of living things the growth of a human being from a baby to an adult
    Effects of gravitational pull free fall, weight
    Natural disasters earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, floods, droughts
    Weather events lightning, thunder, rainbow, aurora, rain, snow

What is the importance of Science in Daily Life?

  1. Our lives and our daily activities are closely related to science. The food we eat, the clothes we wear, the electrical appliances we use are all related to science.

  2. Science helps us to understand ourselves and the environment.

  3. Technology, which means the application of science in daily activities is making our life easier, healthier and more perfect, especially in transportation, agriculture, medicine and communication.

  4. For example,

Fields Use of science
Agriculture The use of technology in agriculture helps to improve the quantity and quality of agricultural products.
Various methods of food processing help to increase our supply of food.
Communication Satellites, computers and the Internet help to make communication easier and faster over long distances.
Daily work The air conditioner, microwave oven and other appliances help to make work easier.
Medicine Discovery of new methods of treatment and medicinal drugs improves our health and lengthens our life expectancy.
Meteorology and climatology Predicting weather and disasters to reduce loss of life and damage to property due to natural disasters.
Natural resources Conservation and preservation of natural resources help to maintain the sustainability of life.
Transport Aeroplanes and bullet trains make long distance travel faster, safer, easier and more comfortable.
The environment Recycling helps to reduce pollution in the environment.

Fields, Careers and Subjects to be Studied in Science

  1. Science is divided into various fields.

  2. Each field involves different, specialised studies.

  3. With the wide range of fields in science, we can choose various careers based on our interest.

  4. Here are some examples:

    Field Study of Subjects Careers
    Astronomy the Moon, stars, planets, the Sun, and other objects in space Astrophysics Astronaut
    Biology living things Biology, zoology, botany, microbiology, physiology Biologist, zoologist, medical doctor, botanist, nurse, genetic researcher
    Chemistry matter and its reactions Chemistry, pharmacology, toxicology, forensics Chemist, pharmacist, biochemist, teacher,forensic scientist
    Geology rocks, soils and minerals Physics, chemistry, geology, geochemistry, geophysics, geomorphology Geologist, Environmentalist, Lecturer, Geophysicist, Geochemist
    Physics characteristics of matter and energy Physics, engineering, electricity Physicist, Engineer, astronomer, meteorologist, lecturer, architect
    Science education science knowledge and educational methodology Biology, chemistry, physics Science teacher, lecturer

Technological Innovation

  1. Innovation in technology has made our life more comfortable and helps us to solve various problem in our daily life.

  2. For example:

Field Subject Example of innovation Use
Agriculture Biology Genetically modified plants To increase the quantity of agricultural produce and food supply for humans
Communication Physics Optical fibre To transfer large amount of data over long distances at high speeds
Construction Chemistry Plastics To produce building materials for various types of daily appliances
Medicine Biology and physics Robotic surgery To perform complex surgical operations
