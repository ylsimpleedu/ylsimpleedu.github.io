1.1 Fields and Careers in Biology

What is Biology?

  1. Biology is the study of living organisms.
  2. The word ‘Biology’ is derived from two Greek words, ‘bios’ which means life and ‘logos’ which means study.
  3. Hence, biology is the study of living things, the environment and the interaction between living things and the environment.

The Contribution of Biology in Everyday Life

  1. The knowledge of biology is widely used in various fields as shown in the table below:
Fields Contribution
Agriculture - Produce high-quality agricultural products
- Clone plants and animals of good strains
- Control and overcome the problem of pests
Industry - Produce nutritious foods
- Introduce food processing and preservation methods
Medicine and public health - Create new medicine to cure diseases
- Invent modern medical equipment and procedures to treat patients
- Invent new vaccines to prevent or treat diseases
Research - Genetic engineering
- Conservation and preservation of endangered species

Development in the Fields of Bology

  1. Biotechnology is a technology that involves the use of living organisms to develop or make chemicals and products. Biotechnology is mainly used in agriculture, food science and medicine. Modern usage includes genetic engineering, cell and tissue culture technologies.

  2. Nanotechnology is a part of science and technology that deals with matter on the atomic and molecular scale
    - things that are about 100 nanometres. In developing countries, nanomedicine is used to help treat diseases and prevent health issues.

  3. Bioinformatics is the application of computer technology to collect, classify, store and analyse biochemical and biological information. It is especially used to help gather and analyse data related to molecular genetics and genetic mapping.

  4. Bioengineering is the application of the principles of engineering and natural sciences to tissues, cells and molecules. It uses the knowledge from working with and manipulating biology to achieve a result that can improve functions in plants and animals. It is used in tissue engineering, biopharmaceutical engineering and genetic engineering.
